Do you know if any other look-a-like domains have been registered and are in use? The Doppelgängers domain discovery service can tell you.

Domain names are unique but can have many subtle variations in spelling and in format, which can easily be overlooked and result in someone visiting the wrong website or emailing to the wrong domain.

The reason's why people on the Internet register and run look-a-like domains are many and varied. However, at the core of all of them is deception. To deceive your visitors and customers into thinking, this website is yours or that this email address is your "real/valid" email address.

The best defence against this happening is knowledge. To know whether any look-a-like domains exist and if they are in use; running a website and/or mail server. Then you will be able to take appropriate steps to mitigate or prevent it from happening.

The Doppelgängers domain discovery service is commissioned by a domain name owner to discover whether or not any look-a-like domain names have been registered and if they are in use.

Upon commissioning a list of candidate domain names is created from variations in TLD/ccTLD's, the insertion, deletion, substitution and transposition of characters of the domain name and common Typo/Domain squatting techniques. The service provides a formal report, including an executive summary, analysis of each individual finding and presents recommendations for consideration.

It is recommended that discovering what look-a-like domains exist be conducted on a regular cycle yearly, quarterly, monthly because of the Internet constantly changing. The Doppelgängers domain discovery service typically takes a week from commissioning to the delivery of the report. email info@chdd.co.uk for more information and discuss the extent of look-a-like variations and possible timings and cost.
